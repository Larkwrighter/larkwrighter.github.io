The Subject

The Subject is the world's smallest escape room.

Taking classic escape room gameplay, compressing it down to an uncomfortably small scale and mixing in a mind-bending narrative, The Subject is a truly unique experience for two or more players.

Description

The Subject is an escape room experience like no other. The game is designed for multiple players; one player enters a small, cramped box, and is locked inside. The other player(s) must solve a series of puzzles to free their teammate. The twist? The information they need to solve the puzzles is on the inside of the box.

In the ultimate test of communication, concentration and calmness under pressure, players must work together to piece together a mysterious narrative, solve the puzzles, and free the subject from the box before their time is up...

The goal of the project is twofold: redefine what escape room games can be, and take them to a new level of intensity.

FAQs

Q. How long does the experience take to play?

A. Players have a maximum time of 30 minutes to complete the game.

Q. But what's the quickest a team has completed it?

A. Just over 19 minutes.

Q. Exactly how big is the box?

The box is roughly 120cm long, 50cm wide and 70cm deep.

Q. More importantly, is the box safe?

Of course. The box was built with player safety in mind, and there are a number of safety procedures in place to ensure that any risks are mitigated.

The box is well ventilated, and a light is provided as part of the experience. The player is able to communicate with those outside throughout the game. A gamemaster is always overseeing the experience whilst a player is inside the box, and they can signal to the gamemaster if they want to get out before the game is over.

The box, whilst secure, is easy to dismantle in the event of a medical emergency. The use of pin hinge fasteners allow the box to be completely taken apart in seconds.

A full and detailed risk assessment is available on request.

Whilst the box is safe, participants who suffer from claustrophobia are advised to avoid entering the box as part of the experience.

Events and Coverage
